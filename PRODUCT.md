# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Two audiences, parents first:

- **Prospective parents** in Kathmandu (primarily Baudha and surrounding areas) deciding where to enroll their child. They arrive skeptical and protective, often on a phone, comparing a handful of schools. Their job to be done: satisfy themselves that this school is safe, proven, and genuinely cares about children before requesting admission info or a visit.
- **Secondary / +2 students** exploring where they'll study. They should feel aspiration and belonging — that this is a place they'd be proud to grow up in.

The page must lead with trust for parents while keeping students inspired underneath. Primary conversion action: the admissions enquiry (request info / book a campus visit).

## Product Purpose

The single landing page for **WITS Academy Secondary School** — Baudha, Kathmandu-6, established 1999, motto *"Education for Civilization."* It exists to convert interest into admission enquiries by conveying, in one scroll, that WITS is an established, values-driven school (25+ years, strong SEE results) that treats each child as an individual. Success = a parent finishing the page trusting the school enough to submit the enquiry form or plan a visit.

## Brand Personality

**Editorial · Refined · Understated.** Quiet confidence over loud salesmanship. The voice is warm but composed — a school that doesn't need to shout because its track record speaks. Copy is plain-spoken and human ("a school that knows each child by name"), never corporate or hype-driven. Emotional goals: trust and reassurance for parents, aspiration and belonging for students. The crest's motto, *Education for Civilization*, is the north star — education as character and citizenship, not just marks.

## Anti-references

- **Generic "AI-generated" SaaS aesthetic** — the reason the first draft was rejected. No rainbow mesh gradients, no glassmorphism-by-default, no gradient text, no purple/blue tech gradients, no identical icon-card grids, no gradient-accented hero-metric templates.
- **Cheap/clip-arty school-website clichés** — stock "diverse students pointing at laptop," garish primary-color blocks, clutter, marquees of logos for their own sake, WordwArt-style headings.
- **Overdesigned/animation-heavy** — motion should feel intentional and calm, never a carousel-and-parallax circus.

## Reference feel (positive)

- **findrealestate.com** — aspirational editorial hero, oversized display type, generous negative space, restrained monochrome with deliberate accent, big stacked type sections.
- **istemlab.ai** — clean light canvas, thin considered UI, big-number stats, the glossy 3D "Our Approach" ring diagram, dark section breaks.

## Design Principles

1. **Restraint reads as prestige.** Warm off-white canvas, near-black ink, forest green as a *deliberate* accent — not everywhere. Whitespace and hairline rules do the work color would.
2. **Show the school is human.** Real faces, named voices, plain warm copy. Every claim ties back to caring for an individual child, not abstract "excellence."
3. **Editorial hierarchy first.** Big characterful display type (Bricolage Grotesque) paired with a clean humanist body (Inter Tight); size and weight carry the hierarchy, not decoration.
4. **Motion with intention.** A few earned moments (the 3D hero tilt, the approach ring, count-ups) that reward attention — each fitted to what it reveals, all reduced-motion safe.
5. **Trust is the conversion.** Every section should move a protective parent one step closer to the enquiry form; the CTA is never more than a scroll away.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target **WCAG 2.1 AA**: body text ≥4.5:1 and large text ≥3:1 against its background (including placeholder text), visible keyboard focus, semantic landmarks and alt text, and a `prefers-reduced-motion` alternative for every animation (the 3D tilt, reveals, count-ups, and marquee must degrade gracefully). Mobile-first: fully usable one-handed on a phone, since many parents will arrive that way.
